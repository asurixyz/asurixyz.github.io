Quantum Machine Learning beyond Kernel Methods

Aug–Nov 2025
Quantum Computing Machine Learning IIT Madras

Course: EE5180, IIT Madras

Compared explicit and implicit (kernel-based) quantum models in a course project, analyzing limits of the Representer Theorem in quantum Hilbert spaces.

  • Designed a Havlíček feature encoding circuit using Z and ZZ gates to embed PCA-compressed Fashion-MNIST data into quantum states
  • Trained models using a PyTorch-based quantum pipeline, achieving test MSE 2.16×10−4 for n = 2, 3
  • Performed scaling analysis for n = 2 to 8, identifying a crossover at n = 4 where explicit models outperform implicit ones by up to 5x
  • Optimized Quantum Kernel Ridge Regression via grid search, showing optimal alpha increases with system size

Spin Systems in Social Networks

Dec 2024
Physics Agent-Based Modeling IMSc Chennai

Workshop: Institute of Mathematical Sciences, Chennai

Simulated Ising spin dynamics on random and modular social networks with up to 360 nodes using the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm.

  • Varied edge sparsity in the 0.25–0.75 range and explored 6 inverse-temperature regimes (beta in [2, 1000])
  • Identified two critical inverse-temperature thresholds (beta approx 2, 10) separating noisy, oscillatory, and stable magnetization regimes
  • Designed and implemented a greedy zealot-placement algorithm on networks of up to 600 nodes, estimating critical zealot fractions in the 0.30–0.45 range
